# Market Making under Inventory Risk — Stochastic Control Approach

This project studies the paper **“Dealing with the Inventory Risk: A Solution to the Market Making Problem”** by Guéant, Lehalle and Fernandez-Tapia. The objective is to understand how stochastic control methods can be used to derive optimal bid and ask quotes for a market maker exposed to inventory risk.

## Project Overview

A market maker continuously posts bid and ask quotes. The strategy generates revenue through the bid-ask spread, but random executions may create a long or short inventory position. This inventory is exposed to price movements and therefore creates financial risk.

The project focuses on the trade-off between:

* increasing execution probability by quoting close to the mid-price;
* earning a larger spread by quoting farther from the mid-price;
* controlling the inventory risk generated by unbalanced executions.

The model is formulated as a stochastic control problem where the market maker dynamically chooses bid and ask quote distances.

## Theoretical Framework

The project covers the following elements:

* modelling of the reference price as a Brownian motion;
* bid and ask quote-distance controls;
* execution processes modelled by point processes;
* exponential execution intensities;
* bounded inventory process;
* CARA utility maximization of terminal mark-to-market wealth;
* Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman formulation;
* change of variables reducing the nonlinear HJB equation to a linear system of ODEs;
* derivation and interpretation of optimal bid and ask quotes.

The key mathematical idea is that the combination of CARA utility and exponential execution intensities allows the original nonlinear HJB problem to be transformed into a finite-dimensional linear ODE system.

## Numerical Implementation

The Python notebook implements the main numerical components of the model:

* construction of the inventory grid;
* computation of the tridiagonal matrix associated with the reduced ODE system;
* resolution of the system using matrix exponentials;
* computation of optimal bid and ask quote surfaces;
* computation of the optimal spread;
* simulation of stochastic price dynamics;
* simulation of inventory trajectories;
* comparison between an inventory-aware optimal strategy and a naive strategy;
* sensitivity analysis with respect to volatility, liquidity and risk aversion;
* illustrative calibration of the execution intensity model using Coinbase BTC-USD data.

## Main Results

The numerical experiments show that the optimal quotes depend strongly on the current inventory level.

When the inventory becomes positive, the strategy makes ask quotes more aggressive in order to encourage selling and reduce exposure. When the inventory becomes negative, the strategy encourages buying in order to move the inventory back toward zero.

The simulations also show that:

* the spread widens for extreme inventory values;
* higher volatility increases the optimal spread;
* higher risk aversion leads to more conservative quotes;
* the inventory-aware strategy produces more stable P&L trajectories than a naive quoting strategy;
* the exponential intensity model can be calibrated illustratively on Coinbase BTC-USD market data.
